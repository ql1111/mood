World Mood Map: a live map of global emotions

Mood2Know is a world mood map that turns simple anonymous mood signals into a live emotional landscape. In one click, people can share how they feel and explore how mood patterns appear across regions, countries, and time zones.

How the world mood map works

Mood2Know is intentionally simple. Participants choose a mood from 0 to 10 and place it on the map using automatic or manual location. That signal is stored anonymously and becomes part of a broader collective emotional view.

Instead of relying on long surveys, social media feeds, or personal storytelling, the project captures a direct and minimal input: one mood, one place, one moment.

What you can see on the map

The experience now goes beyond isolated markers. Mood2Know helps users explore several live indicators of the collective mood:

  • Live world mood map with clustered emotional signals.
  • Global mood score showing the current average mood.
  • Happiest country based on current visible mood patterns.
  • Mood history showing how shared emotions evolve over time.
  • Share options that make it easier to spread the project.

A global emotional snapshot

Emotions are usually experienced privately and remain difficult to observe at a large scale. A world mood map offers another perspective: it reveals a moving collective signal, almost like emotional weather.

Some areas may look calmer, more positive, or more mixed than others. As new moods are added, the map keeps evolving and the broader picture changes with time.

Built for low friction and privacy

Mood2Know is designed to stay lightweight. There is no account, no profile, and no need to write a story. Participation is fast, anonymous, and focused on the collective signal.

Privacy also matters. The map uses approximate rather than overly precise location, helping reveal geographic patterns without exposing exact personal positions. This approach was strengthened through user feedback.

Why this project exists

Mood2Know explores a simple question: can the emotional state of the world be visualized in real time through tiny anonymous contributions?

The answer will never be perfect, but even imperfect signals can reveal something meaningful when they are shared collectively. The project exists to make that invisible layer visible.
