World Mood Map

A world mood map is a way to visualize how people feel across the planet at a given moment. Mood2Know collects simple anonymous mood signals and turns them into a live global emotional landscape.

Instead of long surveys or social media analysis, the idea is intentionally minimal: one question, one click, one moment captured.

Open the world mood map

How the world mood map works

Participants choose a mood between 0 and 10. Their device provides a location, and the data point is placed on the map.

Each point represents a momentary emotional signal. Clusters reveal patterns across cities, countries and time zones.

A global emotional snapshot

Emotions are usually invisible at a collective scale. A world mood map allows us to observe them the same way we observe weather patterns.

Some regions may appear calmer or more positive, while others show more mixed emotional signals. Over time, the map evolves as new moods are shared.

Why this experiment exists

Mood2Know explores a simple question: can we visualize the emotional state of the world in real time?

By keeping participation anonymous and frictionless, the project focuses on the collective signal rather than personal stories.
