What is an emotional weather map?

We already use weather as a shared language: sunny, cloudy, calm, stormy. Mood2Know applies a similar idea to emotions. An emotional weather map is a collective snapshot of how people feel across the world at a given moment, shown through a live global mood map.

Instead of long surveys or complex profiles, Mood2Know asks one simple question: How is your mood, from 0 to 10? With one input, your mood joins an anonymous global signal that can be explored visually.

You can also visit the world mood map to explore the broader live view of global emotions.

How Mood2Know works

  • Choose your mood on a simple 0 to 10 scale.
  • Use automatic location, with manual location as a fallback if needed.
  • Contribute anonymously without creating an account.
  • Explore the map instantly, even before submitting a mood.
  • See live indicators such as the world mood score, mood history, and the happiest country.

Mood2Know is designed to be fast and low-friction: no feed, no profile, no social pressure — just participation and exploration.

What you can explore

Mood2Know is no longer just a map with scattered points. It now helps reveal a broader collective signal through several live indicators:

  • World mood map with clustered emotional signals across regions.
  • Global mood score showing the current average mood.
  • Happiest country based on the latest visible patterns.
  • Mood history to see how the emotional landscape evolves over time.
  • Share tools that make the project easier to spread.

Anonymous and privacy-aware by design

Mood2Know is not a mood diary and not a diagnostic tool. It does not try to explain why you feel a certain way. Its purpose is to make collective emotional patterns visible, not to build personal profiles.

The system stores a simple anonymous signal: mood, approximate location, and an anonymous identifier. No names, no emails, and no public profile are required. Location is intentionally less precise to better protect privacy while still revealing broader geographic patterns.

Why this experiment matters

Most emotions remain invisible at collective scale. Yet at any given moment, millions of people experience joy, fatigue, calm, stress, relief, or uncertainty. A live emotional weather map makes part of that invisible layer visible.

Over time, these emotional signals shift across countries and time zones. What emerges is not a personal story, but a shared human pattern — simple, imperfect, and alive.

Designed to reduce friction

Recent improvements were made directly from user feedback: the map is now visible even before adding a mood, manual geolocation is available, and location is less precise to better respect privacy concerns. These changes make Mood2Know easier to explore, easier to trust, and easier to share.

FAQ

Is Mood2Know a diagnostic or mental health tool?

No. Mood2Know does not diagnose, analyze or interpret individual emotions. It simply visualizes anonymous mood signals at a collective level.

Do I need an account?

No. Participation is intentionally frictionless and does not require account creation.

Can I explore the map before sharing my mood?

Yes. The map is visible even before you contribute, so you can explore the experience first.

Why do you ask for location?

Location helps place each mood on the world map. It can be provided automatically or manually, and is intentionally less precise to better protect privacy.

What data is stored?

A mood value, an approximate location, and an anonymous identifier. No name, no email, and no personal profile are required.

Can I update my mood?

Yes. Submitting again updates your latest mood for your anonymous identifier.

What can I see besides the map?

You can also explore live indicators such as the global mood score, mood history, and the happiest country.
