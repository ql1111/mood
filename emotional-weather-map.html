What is an emotional weather map?

We talk about the weather because it’s a simple shared language: today is sunny, cloudy, stormy. Mood2Know uses the same idea for emotions. An emotional weather map is a collective snapshot of how people feel across the world — right now — displayed as a real-time world mood map.

Instead of long surveys, Mood2Know asks one question: How is your mood, from 0 to 10? You answer in one click, anonymously, and the map reveals aggregated moods from other participants.

You can also explore the world mood map to see how global emotions appear across different regions.

How Mood2Know works

  • Pick a mood (0 to 10).
  • Locate + Save (your device provides a location).
  • See the world’s mood on a live map with clustered markers.

Mood2Know is designed to be lightweight: no account, no feed, no social pressure — just participation.

Anonymous by design

Mood2Know is not a mood diary. It is not therapy and does not attempt to explain why you feel a certain way. The goal is simply to make a collective emotional signal visible.

The system stores a simple data point: mood, approximate location and an anonymous identifier. No names, no emails and no profiles.

Why this experiment matters

Most emotions remain invisible. Yet at any moment millions of people are experiencing joy, stress, calm or uncertainty. A global emotional map helps visualize this invisible layer of human experience.

Over time the emotional weather shifts across countries and time zones, revealing how collective mood evolves around the world.

FAQ

Is Mood2Know a diagnostic tool?

No. Mood2Know does not analyze or diagnose emotions. It simply visualizes anonymous mood signals.

Do I need an account?

No account is required. Participation is intentionally frictionless.

Why do you ask for location?

The map needs a location to place each mood on the global map.

Can I update my mood?

Yes. Submitting again updates your latest mood for your anonymous identifier.
